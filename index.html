    Real noob mode

    Tutorial & FAQ

    This page explains Signal Floor slowly, with examples. You do not need to know betting, finance, trading, or probability math. If you can answer “do I think this will happen?”, you can play.

    The basic idea

    Every market is just a yes/no question.

    business Will Sales hit the April ARR target?
    64% YES

    Question

    A good market asks something clear enough that everyone understands what YES means.

    Your opinion

    Buy YES if you think it will happen. Buy NO if you think it will not happen.

    The signal

    If people become more confident, the probability rises. If confidence fades, it falls.

    Example round

    How one bet plays out.

    1 A market is created

    “Will we announce a new customer logo before the next all-hands?”

    2 You spend 100 credits on YES

    The YES probability moves up slightly because your trade shows confidence.

    3 Other people react

    Someone heard the opposite and buys NO. The probability drops. Now the rumor has a visible price.

    4 The market resolves

    If the customer is announced, YES wins. If not, NO wins. Void means stakes are returned.

    Good bets

    Use this recipe.

    Specific outcome ARR, sales, product, hiring, culture, office lore, customer wins.
    Deadline A concrete date keeps it exciting and resolvable.
    Resolution source All-hands, CRM, finance sheet, Slack announcement, founder statement, visible event.
    Safe framing No personal attacks, private HR topics, health, salary, or protected characteristics.
    Good Will Sales reach the official April ARR target by April 30?

    Clear outcome, clear deadline, clear resolution source.

    Too vague Is Sales doing well?

    No deadline, no single source, and people will argue about what “well” means.

    FAQ

    Common questions.

    Is this real money?

    No. The prototype uses credits only. Credits are for competition and learning, not deposits or cash-out.

    Can founders see who I am?

    The app is designed around pseudonymous names. In this prototype, identities are local browser personas, not public company identities.

    Why does the probability change?

    Because trades move the market. More YES buying pushes the YES probability up. More NO buying pushes it down.

    What happens when a market ends?

    The admin resolves it as YES, NO, or Void. Winning positions receive credits. Void returns stakes.

    What should we avoid?

    Keep sensitive markets admin-approved, clearly opt-in where needed, and focused on public company-context signals. No protected characteristics, health, salary, or personal attacks.
